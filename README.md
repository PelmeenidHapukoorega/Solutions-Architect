#### Passed AZ-104 today with first try

Bit of a background: I have a degree as IT Systems specialist from a vocational education school. Never ended up working in IT due to life and the choices I made. 
Worked in sales since graduating for 6 years until last year I decided to quit sales point blank and got back into IT since I've always loved it and have always tinkered at home.

For AZ-104 I started studying this year January. At first I figured I'll pass it in a month's time, until the deeper I dived into it the more I realised how it's not gonna happen.

My study approach for AZ-104:

I did all the official Microsoft labs for AZ-104, which in hindsight didn't cover all that much that you would need to know. 
I didn't use the official learn path for AZ-104 since I considered it all theoretical, and I consider the best practice method to genuinely build stuff yourself and then read up on it 
i.e. set a goal that you want to see happen, and just start building it, messed up A LOT then looked up the errors, what those meant, what was missing and went from there.

Used John Savill's 104 playlist as well as exam cram. Used it in between: if I was tired of doing labs I switched to Savill, reading and watching YT guides and vice versa.

For practice exams I used MeasureUp and TD practice exams. For MeasureUp I first scored around 70% which was in March. 
Eventually started to score over 85% but also realised I was starting to memorize questions and wasn't actually focusing on how it worked.

Then I started using Claude for teaching me the scenarios I used from practice exams and told it to teach me the concepts of it. 
I then told it to provide me with labs from those scenarios and mix them up in order to avoid memorization, and honestly I gotta say that taught me a lot. 
Using Claude for labs genuinely helped me the most as to actually understand how each piece fit together, but still read up on the best practices anyway and tried to do things my way.

For cmdlets and such I never outright copy pasted anything, wrote out everything myself as to not to cheese and at first understand what I was typing, doing and achieving. 
Once I felt more comfortable with that it started to come off as muscle memory and I then moved on to IaC.

Practice exam progression:

After doing this method for almost 2 months, I started doing TD practice exams again. Went straight for timed mode and got the first 3 sets around 74%, 4th set around 51% and 5th bonus set around 60%.

After, I filtered for wrong answers and answers I felt unsure of, fed them to Claude and told it to provide me drill labs for my specific gaps. 
After each lab Claude gave me around 10-15 scenario questions about concepts covered in the drill labs.

Waited for 4-5 days before trying another timed set round, and this time scored on all 4 over 86% and set 5 at 100% (6 questions only). That was 2 days ago, and today I actually passed.

Resources used:

Exam takeaways:

Do not underestimate it and actually put in the work.

Nothing prepares you for it, you wont feel ready for it, you just have to pull the trigger on it (You will know your gaps to improve)

Honestly the biggest form of advice I can give anyone here about how to pass AZ-104 is to just build stuff in and around of Azure, like thats by far the most important thing about passing it. 
You can theorize and visually picture whatever you like but thats assumption not understanding.

Practice tests, exams, docs and whatever else is completely useless if you havent even touched the thing.
